Public Prosecutor v Tan Kim Ping

Public Prosecutor v Tan Kim Ping
[2021] SGDC 10

Case Number : District Arrest Case No 911988 of 2020 and others, Magistrate's Appeals No. 9018/2021/01
Decision Date : 27 January 2021
Tribunal/Court : District Court
Coram : Soh Tze Bian
Counsel Name(s) : SPO S Krishnan for the Prosecution; Accused in person.
Parties : Public Prosecutor — Tan Kim Ping

Criminal Law – Drug charges of possession and consumption – Sentence – Home detention order

[LawNet Editorial Note: The appeal in MA 9018/2021/01 was withdrawn on 27 January 2021.]

27 January 2021

District Judge Soh Tze Bian:

CHARGES

1       The accused person, Tan Kim Ping (“AP”) pleaded guilty to 2 drug charges of possession and consumption as follows:

(a)      DAC-911988-2020 : On 20 May 2020 at about 11:10 pm, outside the Aljunied MRT Station near Lorong 25 Geylang, Singapore, the AP did have in his possession a Class A controlled drug listed in the First Schedule to the Misuse of Drugs Act (Cap 185, 2008 Rev Ed ) (“MDA”), to wit, two (2) packets containing not less than 0.67 gram of crystalline substance which was pulverized and homogenised into a powdery substance and which was analysed and found to contain not less than 0.45 gram of methamphetamine, without authorisation under the MDA or the Regulations made thereunder, and he has thereby committed an offence under section 8(a) and punishable under section 33(1) of the MDA with imprisonment up to 10 years’ imprisonment or $20,000/- fine, or both.

2        DAC-911989-2020: On or before 20 May 2020, in Singapore, the AP did consume a Specified Drug listed in the Fourth Schedule to the Misuse of Drugs Act, (Cap 185, 2008 Rev Ed), to wit, methamphetamine, without any authorisation under the said Act or the Regulations made there under, and he has thereby committed an offence under Section 8(b)(ii) and punishable under Section 33(3A) of the Misuse of Drugs Act (Cap 185, 2008) Rev Ed) with a prescribed minimum sentence of 1 year’s imprisonment and up to a maximum sentence of 10 years’ imprisonment or $20,000 fine, or both.

3       The AP also consented to 2 other drug-related charges in DAC-911990-2020 of having drug utensils in his possession, and in DAC-919306-2020 of having consumed a Class A controlled drug listed in the First Schedule to the Misuse of Drugs Act, (Cap 185, 2008 Rev Ed), to wit, Gamma Hydroxybutyric Acid (GHB), to be taken into consideration for the purpose of sentence for the 2 proceeded charges in DAC-911988-2020 & DAC-911989-2020. (hereinafter referred to as “TIC charges”).

STATEMENT OF FACTS

4       The statement of facts which the AP had admitted without qualification stated as follows:

“1    The accused is Tan Kim Ping, a 35-year-old male/Malaysian, (Singapore Permanent Resident) and bearer of NRIC No: SXXXXXXXH. At the material time, he was working as a freelance programmer.

2    On 20 May 2020 at about 11.10 pm, the accused was arrested by police officers from the Bedok Police Divisional Headquarters outside the Aljunied MRT Station near Lorong 25 Geylang, Singapore for drug related offences. He was found in possession of the following item: two packets of crystalline substance which was later marked as “TKP-03”.

FACTS PERTAINING TO THE 1ST CHARGE [DAC-911988-2020] DRUG POSSESSION

3    On 22 May 2020, the seized exhibit marked as “TKP-03” was placed in a tamper-proof bag and submitted to the Health Sciences Authority (“HSA”) for analysis.

4    On 10 June 2020, Dr See Kum Foong Larissa, an analyst from the Illicit Drugs Laboratory of the HSA issued a certificate under s 16 of the Misuse of Drugs Act, Chapter 185 (2008 Rev Ed) (“MDA”) bearing Lab. Number. ID-2032-00849-001, stating that the exhibit marked “TKP-03” was found to be two packets containing not less than 0.67 g of crystalline substance which was pulverised and homogenised into a powdery substance. The powdery substance was analysed and found to contain not less than 0.45g of methamphetamine.

5     Methamphetamine is a Class A Controlled Drug listed in the First Schedule to the MDA.

6    The accused admitted to the possession and knowledge of the exhibit marked “TKP-03” and stated that it was meant for his own consumption. The accused admitted that he bought 6.25g of ‘ice” (street name for methamphetamine) from an unidentified male Indian called “Bored” at Serangoon Avenue 1 for $600 either in March or April 2020.

7    The accused is not authorised to be in possession of methamphetamine under the said Act or the Regulations made thereunder, and he has thereby committed an offence under Section 8(a) and punishable under s 33(1) of the MDA.

FACTS PERTAINING TO THE 2ND CHARGE [DAC-911989 2020] DRUG CONSUMPTION

8    On 21 May 2020, the accused provided samples of his urine in 2 bottles which were marked and sealed in his presence and sent to the HSA on 21 May 2020 for analysis.

9    Upon analysis, on 26 May 2020, the HSA issued two certificates as follows:

a.    Zhang Hui Fen Hannah, an analyst from the HSA, having conducted the analysis in relation to the urine sample marked ‘P-SG-20-00575-1 TAN KIM PING SXXXXXXXH’, issued the certificate bearing Lab. No. AT-2033-02941-001-03 stating that on analysis, the said urine sample was found to contain methamphetamine.

b.    Chang Hian Twan, an analyst from the HSA, having conducted the analysis in relation to the urine sample marked ‘P-SG-20-00575-2 TAN KIM PING SXXXXXXXH’’, issued the certificate bearing Lab No. AT-2033-02941-002-03, stating that on analysis, the said urine sample was found to contain methamphetamine.

10     Methamphetamine is a Specified Drug listed in the Fourth Schedule to the MDA.

11    The accused admitted that he smoked “ice” since August or September 2019. He admitted last smoking “ice” prior to his arrest on 20 May 20 with a friend at a hotel along Beach Road. The accused admitted that he smoked ‘ice” by pouring some “ice” into an improvised glass tube. He then heated the base of the glass tube and inhaled the fumes emitted using an improvised straw attached to the improvised glass tube. The accused’s urine samples were found to contain methamphetamine (as stated in paragraph 9) as a result of the accused knowingly having smoked “ice”.

12    The accused is not authorised under the MDA or the Regulations made thereunder to consume a Specified Drug and he has thereby committed an offence under the s 8(b)(ii) and punishable under s 33(3A) of the MDA.”

PROSECUTION SUBMISSION ON SENTENCE

5       The prosecution submitted for a sentence of 8 to 12 months for the charge in DAC-911988-2020 and for the prescribed minimum sentence of 1 year’s imprisonment for the charge in DAC-911989-2020 and for both sentences to run concurrently.

MITIGATION PLEA

6       In his mitigation plea, the AP pleaded for a lenient sentence and submitted, inter alia, as follows:

“I'm truly remorseful for my actions. When I was arrested, I had a revelation and it was the wake up call that I needed. From that moment, I knew I had to change my ways and have to be better. I was very cooperative with my arresting officers and immediately confessed and came clean to my Investigating Officers. And now here I am readily admitting to my actions and pleading guilty to this court. Then I was remanded too for one night and that made me even more determined to turn a new leaf. I had made efforts to kick this habit and also made key lifestyle changes. I have cut off those who have a bad influence on me. I have even attended 3 support group meetings and plan on intending to do it on a weekly/biweekly basis. And to keep myself occupied, I focused even more on work and invested more time on the things that interest me like lego building and immerse myself into gardening and plant collecting (mainly from the aroids family). I have converted portions of my place of residents into a truly lush tropical jungle.

My plea mitigation today is to persuade the court to be lenient on my sentencing by imposing a Home Detention Order in lieu of jail sentence. Or any other sentence that still allows me to keep on working. The reason why I ask this is because, I...

- can keep paying my rent and maintain my place of residence

- can continue servicing my bank loan

- can still support my parents

- can pay for my father's treatment (took additional loan for treatment)

- can serve my existing 50,000 clients and especially the 700 premium clients. (i run a

subscription based SaaS)

- can maintain my server farm of 15 servers that hosts my client's websites & my services.

- and at the same time take care of my plants.

I will comply with all the conditions of my house arrest.

- wear any electronic monitoring device 24/7

- attend all rehabilitation/corrective programs to be better.

- report back whenever I'm asked adhocly

- provide my urine for testing.

And if I don't comply, Your Honor can always kick me back in.” (emphasis mine)

REASONS FOR THE SENTENCE

7       I note from the AP’s mitigation plea that the AP is remorseful from his cooperation with investigations and plea of guilt, and also appeared to have made various lifestyle changes to kick his drug habits and cut himself off from those who have a bad influence on him.

8       However, I am unable to consider the AP’s submission for a home detention order which is outside the sentencing power of the courts. The power to order home detention is only conferred on the Commissioner of Prisons under section 52 of the Prisons Act (Cap 247) which empowers the Commissioner, for the purpose of facilitating a prisoner’s rehabilitation and reintegration into society, to order the release of a prisoner who is eligible for home detention for a period not exceeding 12 months (or such other period as the Minister may, by notification in the Gazette, prescribe in substitution thereof) based on the prescribed criteria under section 53 of the Prisons Act (Cap 247).

9       At the hearing for his post sentence bail on 22 January 2021, the AP informed the Court that he was seeking admission to a drug rehabilitation centre (DRC) in his appeal filed against the sentence. However, such DRC admission is outside the sentencing power of the courts. The power to send a drug abuser to a DRC is vested in the Director of the Central Narcotics Bureau under section 34 of the MDA.

10     While the AP has no prior drug or other antecedents, he faced a total of 4 charges under the MDA with 2 proceeded charges and 2 charges taken into consideration for sentencing. All the drugs involved in the 2 proceeded charges were Class A controlled drugs. In this regard, I had to bear in mind what was stated by the High Court in Dinesh Singh Bhatia v PP [2005] 3 SLR 1 at [59] as follows:

“It must now be clearly and unfailingly understood that all drug offences involving the possession or consumption of Class A drugs inexorably attract custodial sentences, save in purely exceptional cases.”

11     As for the charge of drug possession in DAC-911988-2020, the sentencing benchmark range for a first time offender for possession of a class A controlled drug (such as Methamphetamine) is between 6 to 18 months’ imprisonment (see Nathan Sandy Darren v PP [2007] SGDC 275; and PP v Choi Byeongkuk [2011] SGHC 6). In relation to the 2nd charge of drug consumption in DAC-911989-2020, the prescribed statutory minimum sentence is 1 year’s imprisonment. As all drug offences involving the possession or consumption of Class A drugs must inexorably attract custodial sentences, (save in a purely exceptional case for which I found none in the present case), I am unable to impose any other sentence on the AP which will allow him to keep on working and pay his bills, as submitted by the AP in his mitigation plea.

12     As for the AP’s personal financial hardship occasioned by an imprisonment term, this cannot be a mitigating factor if it arose from the AP’s own acts.[note: 1] Hardship was within presage and the offender had brought it upon himself.[note: 2] Personal ruin is what one must expect when one embarks on a life of crime.[note: 3] Hardship to the AP’s family is not a strong factor that can be relied upon in mitigation of an offence, save possibly in the most exceptional or extreme of circumstances and this was solely lacking in the present case[note: 4].

13     Having to regard to all the relevant circumstances of the present case, including the AP’s mitigation plea and the sentencing considerations and benchmarks, I agreed with the prosecution submission for a concurrent sentence of at least 8 months for the charge in DAC-911988-2020 and for the prescribed minimum sentence of 1 year’s imprisonment for the charge in DAC-911989-2020.

CONCLUSION

14     Accordingly, I imposed on the AP a sentence of 8 months imprisonment for the charge in DAC-911988-2020 and the prescribed minimum sentence of 1 year’s imprisonment for the charge in DAC-911989-2020, and I ordered both sentences to run concurrently with a global sentence of 1 year’s imprisonment. The global sentence of 1 year’s imprisonment was not manifestly excessive or inadequate, but wholly appropriate and commensurate with the nature and extent of the AP’s culpability and was a sufficient deterrent sentence tempered with proportionality without the effect of a crushing sentence.

15     Dissatisfied with my decision, the AP had filed a notice of appeal against his sentence on 18 January 2021, but at the hearing for his post sentence bail on 22 January 2021, the AP informed the Court that he would withdraw his appeal which he did so on 27 January 2021.
